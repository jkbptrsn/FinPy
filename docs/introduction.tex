\section{Definitions}

The price of a zero-coupon bond maturing at time $t$, $P(0,t)$, is related to the continuously compounded yield
\begin{equation}
P(0,t) = \e^{-y(0,0,t) t}.
\end{equation}

\begin{equation}
P(0,t) = \exp \left( -\int_0^t f(0,u) du \right).
\end{equation}
Thus
\begin{equation}
f(t,T) = - \frac{\partial \ln P(t,T)}{\partial T}.
\end{equation}
